# CoverGuard — Sales Agent Positioning Playbook

**Audience:** Independent P&C insurance Sales Agents and small agency principals.
**Purpose:** How to position, sell, and operationalize CoverGuard so it produces bound policies, not just reports.

---

## 1. The Frame: Move from "Last Person in the Deal" to "First"

Today most P&C agents enter a transaction late — after the buyer has accepted an offer, often within ten days of closing. By then the property is already chosen, the loan is in motion, and the agent's only job is to find a carrier that will write whatever was bought. In hardening markets, that frequently means scrambling, calling six carriers, and watching deals fall apart.

CoverGuard repositions the agent. With a property address, the agent can pull a multi-peril risk report, see which of 150+ carriers will quote that ZIP, and produce a binding-ready quote in 90 seconds. That capability turns the agent into a **pre-offer advisor**, not a last-minute fixer.

The pitch to the realtor and the lender is simple: "Send me the address before they make an offer. I'll tell you in two minutes whether it's insurable, who will write it, and what it will cost."

## 2. The Three Hero Use Cases

### a. The Pre-Offer Insurability Check

- **Trigger:** Realtor or buyer evaluating a property.
- **Agent action:** Run CoverGuard report, share with realtor and buyer.
- **Outcome:** Agent becomes the realtor's go-to. Every future deal flows through them.

### b. The Carrier Exit Save

- **Trigger:** Carrier non-renewal or new market exit (e.g., State Farm in CA, Citizens depopulation in FL).
- **Agent action:** Use carrier-availability map to identify replacement carriers writing that ZIP.
- **Outcome:** Retention save. Re-shop becomes a 10-minute task instead of a 2-day phone tree.

### c. The Lender Insurability Hand-Off

- **Trigger:** Loan officer pre-approval.
- **Agent action:** Embed a CoverGuard report into the LO's pre-approval packet. Flag uninsurable properties before underwriting wastes time.
- **Outcome:** Lender refers every borrower to the agent.

## 3. Four Buyer Personas the Agent Sells Through

| Persona | Their need | Agent angle |
|---|---|---|
| Realtor | Stop losing deals at the closing table | "I'll tell you in 2 min if your listing is insurable" |
| Loan Officer | Avoid wasted underwriting hours | Pre-approval insurability gate |
| Buyer | Confidence before earnest money | Free CoverGuard report co-branded with agent |
| Mortgage broker | Loan referral conversion | Bundle insurance referral + close-on-time guarantee |

## 4. The 60-Second Cold Pitch (script)

> "I'm with [Agency]. We're working with realtors and loan officers in your area on a new tool that tells you in two minutes whether a property is even insurable — flood, fire, wind, the carrier exits, all of it. It's free for you to send me an address. The first time I save you from a closing-day insurance fire drill, you'll never want to work without it. Want me to run a property for you right now?"

## 5. Email Cadence (3-touch)

### Touch 1 — Realtor partner ask

> Subject: "2-minute insurability check on your next listing"
> Body: short, anchors on a recent named carrier exit, offers free pre-offer report, ends with a CTA to send one address.

### Touch 2 — Case study

> Subject: "How [Agent X] saved a $640K deal in Tampa"
> Body: Anonymized story of carrier-exit save with screenshot of the carrier-availability map.

### Touch 3 — Embedded value loop

> Subject: "I just pulled a report on 1234 Main"
> Body: Personalized — agent has actually run a CoverGuard report on a current MLS listing and shares findings unprompted.

## 6. Discovery Questions (qualify a Sales Agent's fit)

1. How often does insurability surface as a closing-day issue for your book?
2. Which carriers have you lost appointments with in the last 12 months?
3. What does your re-shop motion look like when a non-renewal hits?
4. How are you sourcing new business today — is it referral-led?
5. Do you have a working relationship with realtors and loan officers, or are you primarily inbound?

## 7. Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I already have rater X." | "Raters tell you price. CoverGuard tells you whether the carrier is even open in that ZIP — and gives you a multi-peril risk story you can hand the buyer." |
| "Realtors don't pay me to do this." | "Right — they refer you. The free report is the door-opener. It's not the business model, it's the lead gen." |
| "I don't have time to run reports." | "Two-minute self-serve. You can run it from your phone walking out of an open house." |
| "How is this different from HazardHub?" | "HazardHub is for underwriters. CoverGuard is for you, the producer — same risk depth, plus carrier availability and a quote path." |

## 8. KPI Dashboard for Sales Agents

The platform should expose, for each agent:

- Reports run / week
- Reports → quote conversion rate
- Quote → bind conversion rate
- Average days from address-pulled to bind
- Realtor and LO referral source attribution
- Re-shop saves (renewals retained after a non-renewal flag)

## 9. Marketing Angles by Channel

- **LinkedIn:** Thought-leadership posts on carrier exits, "deal-saver" stories.
- **Local realtor/MLS associations:** Lunch-and-learn webinars co-branded with realtor boards.
- **Lender partnerships:** Embed CoverGuard report as a pre-approval requirement.
- **Search:** "Is my property insurable" long-tail SEO funnel that hands the lead to the local agent.
- **Podcast circuit:** Insurance Journal, IA Magazine, RIS Media (real estate trade).
